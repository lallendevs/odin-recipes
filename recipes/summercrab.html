Summer Crab Salad

Home

Description

Light, cool, and refreshing, this summer crab salad celebrates the sweetness of fresh crab with crisp vegetables and a creamy, tangy dressing. It’s the kind of dish that doesn’t need to be fussy—simple ingredients come together quickly to create something that feels both indulgent and refreshing. Perfect for hot afternoons, cookouts, or an easy make-ahead lunch, it’s a dish that lets the crab shine without being weighed down.

The dressing blends mayonnaise and cocktail sauce with lemon juice and garlic, striking a balance between creamy, tangy, and just a little zesty. Crisp pickles, juicy tomatoes, and sharp red onion cut through the richness, giving the salad crunch and brightness. Every bite combines sweet seafood with pops of acid and savory depth.

Serve it spooned onto lettuce leaves, piled high on a toasted bun, or simply chilled in a bowl with a wedge of lemon on the side. However you enjoy it, this crab salad captures the flavor of summer in its simplest, most satisfying form.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh lump crabmeat, picked over for shells
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 3 tablespoon cocktail sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup diced pickles (or dill relish)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered (or diced regular tomatoes)
  • 1/2 cup finely diced red onion
  • Fresh parsley or dill, chopped (optional, for garnish)
  • Lettuce leaves or toasted rolls, optional for serving

Directions

  1. Prepare the dressing: In a large bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, cocktail sauce, lemon juice, and garlic until smooth.
  2. Add vegetables: Stir in diced pickles, tomatoes, and red onion. Mix until evenly coated.
  3. Fold in crab: Gently add crabmeat to the bowl, folding carefully to avoid breaking up the lumps too much. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes to let flavors meld.
  5. Serve: Spoon onto lettuce leaves for a light option, or pile onto toasted rolls for a hearty sandwich. Garnish with fresh parsley or dill and serve with lemon wedges.
